# Domain 6: Preparedness & Response (Proposals #134-175)

## Biological Risk Mitigation: Generic Measures

### #134 - CBRN Access Control
**Goal:** Address AI's capacity to lower barriers to Chemical, Biological, Radiological and Nuclear weapons through specialized restrictions.
**Mechanism:** Investments in infrastructure and personnel to establish comprehensive pandemic detection systems, strengthening traditional safeguards like wastewater sequencing, vaccine stockpiling, and securing critical supply chains.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #135 - Medical Countermeasures
**Goal:** Develop broad-spectrum medical interventions that protect against entire classes of pathogens rather than specific strains.
**Mechanism:** Direct significant funding toward rapid response platforms that can quickly produce targeted countermeasures when novel threats emerge; create stockpiles of key generic countermeasures ahead of time.
**Who's working on it:** Existing research momentum mentioned but requires substantially increased scale.

### #136 - UV Technology
**Goal:** Develop Far-UVC light technology for continuous environmental sanitization without risks associated with conventional UV systems.
**Mechanism:** Safety testing proceeds rapidly while simultaneously building production capacity and stockpiles for deployment during serious outbreaks; navigate regulatory hurdles through specific use-case authorizations or emergency provisions.
**Who's working on it:** Columbia University Irving Medical Center research referenced.

### #137 - Nucleic Acid Observatory
**Goal:** Significantly enhance global biosurveillance capabilities for early detection of emerging pathogens or deliberately engineered biological threats.
**Mechanism:** Expand the Nucleic Acid Observatory concept with multiple teams pursuing different technical approaches; deploy complementary monitoring systems across strategic locations with centralized international coordination.
**Who's working on it:** Nucleic Acid Observatory (naobservatory.org).

### #138 - Biological Countermeasures
**Goal:** Provide confidence that biological risks accelerated by AI capabilities can be effectively contained and mitigated.
**Mechanism:** Comprehensive biological threat assessment, preparation of defensive countermeasures, and rapid response capabilities working across public health systems, research institutions, and biotechnology development.
**Who's working on it:** Alvea Corp mentioned as model approach.

## Biological Risk Mitigation: AI-Specific Measures

### #139 - Biosecurity Controls
**Goal:** Implement strong controls over sensitive data that could enable harmful applications, particularly for biological and dual-use technologies.
**Mechanism:** Develop methods to "train out this data, exclude it, grep it against the data source"; implement robust KYC guidelines across the stack; develop specific detection mechanisms for biology-related queries; full-specification models only available to licensed institutions.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #140 - Biorisk Thresholds for Open Models
**Goal:** Establish the conditional impacts of specific AI capabilities on biological risks through empirical work.
**Mechanism:** Conduct wet lab studies to quantify how AI capabilities demonstrated in benchmarks translate to real-world risk increases.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #141 - Global Immune System
**Goal:** Create an early warning biodefense ecosystem using next-generation wearables for host response-based diagnosis.
**Mechanism:** Develop wearables that detect physiological anomalies indicating infection even without identifying the specific pathogen; identify additional measurements beyond current capabilities; operationalize existing devices into a distributed global immune system.
**Who's working on it:** Leverages existing wearable technology ecosystem.

### #142 - AI for Bio-resilience
**Goal:** Strengthen societal defenses against AI-enabled misuse, particularly for biological weapons and cyber threats.
**Mechanism:** Develop specific technological safeguards, monitoring systems, and response protocols for the most concerning misuse vectors, prioritizing those that could emerge within 12-24 months.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #143 - Physical Security Mechanisms
**Goal:** Develop physical security measures that constrain bioweapons at the material level rather than focusing on software-based solutions.
**Mechanism:** Develop self-replicating vaccines that transmit like normal viruses as potential solution for novel bio-threats; fund experts who take near-future AI capabilities seriously; move beyond current biological literature for defensive technologies.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

## Democratic & Societal Resilience

### #144 - Resilience Research
**Goal:** Scale research capacity to develop coherent resilience frameworks tailored to different regional vulnerabilities.
**Mechanism:** Address knowledge gaps in critical preparedness areas with research topics ranging from alternative food production to critical infrastructure protection.
**Who's working on it:** ALLFED, Matt Boyd's research group (Adapt Research Ltd.) in New Zealand, Penn State's nuclear winter research program.

### #145 - Civilizational Resilience Measures
**Goal:** Enhance society's overall ability to withstand disruptive AI impacts by strengthening society's "immune system."
**Mechanism:** Secure essential systems from cyberattacks; improve detection and response capabilities for novel biological threats; develop protocols for responsible transfer of decision-making to AI systems; create redundant systems; leverage AI for improved defensive capabilities.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #146 - Large Group Deliberation Systems
**Goal:** Maintain distributed power in the face of rapid AI advancement through collective decision-making systems.
**Mechanism:** Develop tools involving extensive testing with real humans to iteratively improve understanding of effective processes, enabling quick and efficient engagement when important decisions need to be made.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #147 - Anti-Goodharting Tooling
**Goal:** Create robust verification systems where AI-generated plans and analyses are automatically cross-referenced and checked.
**Mechanism:** Have several different AI instances doing deep research to provide reports that are automatically cross-examined to highlight missing perspectives; create a "first version of scalable bureaucracy" with humans reviewing critical decisions.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #148 - Citizen Assemblies for Global Catastrophic Risks
**Goal:** Distill complex ideas into implementable solutions while maintaining legitimacy through representative public involvement.
**Mechanism:** Ordinary citizens receive expert briefings and engage in extended deliberation on complex issues, resulting in outcomes that enjoy broader public acceptance than top-down approaches.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

## Economic Transition Planning

### #149 - Economic Transition
**Goal:** Plan for AI-driven economic transformation to maintain stability as automation potentially displaces human labor at unprecedented scale.
**Mechanism:** Develop both technical systems and policy frameworks addressing how economic value and ownership will be distributed, considering universal basic income, stake-based ownership models, and rethinking of work itself.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #150 - Human Productivity
**Goal:** Help humans remain economically productive in an AI-dominated economy through augmentation rather than replacement.
**Mechanism:** Create augmentation systems that leverage uniquely human capabilities while using AI to enhance productivity; design interfaces that amplify human judgment, creativity, and supervision capabilities.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #151 - Economic Resilience
**Goal:** Address anticipated economic disruption from AI-driven displacement expected within a 3-5 year timeframe.
**Mechanism:** Redesign social contracts and economic structures to account for radically different AI-driven productivity dynamics through advance preparation rather than reactive measures.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #152 - AI Economy Infrastructure
**Goal:** Build infrastructure and protocols for an AI-to-AI economy while ensuring alignment considerations are fundamentally integrated.
**Mechanism:** Prepare for a near-future economy where AI systems primarily transact with other AI systems; create "permanent hackathon" teams continuously building AI products financially supported by successful deployments.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #153 - AI Displacement Response Planning
**Goal:** Develop comprehensive approaches to address workforce displacement from AI advancement.
**Mechanism:** Create frameworks for identifying vulnerable sectors, quantifying impact timelines, and implementing retraining programs before mass displacement occurs; engage government agencies, educational institutions, industry leaders, and labor organizations.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #154 - Post-AGI Society Planning
**Goal:** Develop concrete plans for what society would look like after the development of AGI.
**Mechanism:** Coordinate large-scale effort with governmental backing to establish agreed-upon frameworks for managing the transition; include consideration of "extreme plans" and honest assessments addressing both upside and downside cases.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

## Crisis Response Planning

### #155 - Attack Scenarios Analysis
**Goal:** Develop and publish detailed, evidence-based analyses of plausible catastrophic scenarios.
**Mechanism:** Conduct professional wargaming exercises that bring together key stakeholders to explore scenarios methodically, creating shared understanding among the core 500-1,000 people who need this knowledge.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #156 - Backup Planning
**Goal:** Design practical implementation guidelines for maintaining essential services during catastrophic disruptions.
**Mechanism:** Integrate local food systems, infrastructure capabilities, political contexts, and population needs into contingency plans for scenarios where AI deployment might catalyze catastrophic events.
**Who's working on it:** Similar to ALLFED but with AI-specific focus.

### #157 - Build the Off Button
**Goal:** Build a universal "off button" as a critical line of defense when all other safety layers fail.
**Mechanism:** Develop emergency shutdown capabilities for systems potentially smarter than humans when containment measures fail.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #158 - Food Security
**Goal:** Prepare for disruption of global food networks and conventional agriculture becoming less available.
**Mechanism:** Develop seaweed cultivation and make it accessible in areas with suitable climates but no relevant expertise (e.g., Nigeria); create specialized seedbanks; convert industrial facilities like paper mills to emergency food production through cellulose processing.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

## Cybersecurity Measures

### #159 - Model Weight Security
**Goal:** Strengthen security of model weights across data centers and critical infrastructure to prevent catastrophic leaks.
**Mechanism:** Ensure security implementations are deployed at data centers and other places where model weights are stored.
**Who's working on it:** Various labs and security groups pushing elements forward; best practices exist but unevenly implemented.

### #160 - AI Lab Security
**Goal:** Improve high-assurance security across AI labs by pushing toward consistent baselines.
**Mechanism:** Gain management buy-in; recruit specialized security talent; develop lab-specific security measures; align with emerging frontier-lab and NIST-inspired security expectations.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #161 - Defense-in-Depth for Closed Source Models
**Goal:** Make closed source models harder to misuse through "Swiss cheese layered" defense approaches.
**Mechanism:** Create multiple protective barriers that collectively strengthen security while raising difficulty levels to create meaningful deterrence.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #162 - AI-Enhanced Defense Mechanisms
**Goal:** Leverage AI systems as part of defensive infrastructure to scale protective capabilities alongside offensive capabilities.
**Mechanism:** Develop AI-powered defensive systems with alignment-focused funders who won't derail development with premature commercialization pressures.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #163 - SL5 Data Center Pilots
**Goal:** Run pilot programs for SL5-grade protocols specifically designed for frontier AI systems.
**Mechanism:** Develop practical security measures while reducing operational costs; create testing grounds for security frameworks that could become industry standards. (SL5 resists "top-priority operations by the world's most capable nation-state actors.")
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #164 - Vulnerability Patching Systems
**Goal:** Develop enhanced mechanisms for rapidly identifying and addressing software vulnerabilities before exploitation by AI systems.
**Mechanism:** Create incentives for vulnerability disclosure; develop automated detection of potential security gaps; improve deployment of patches across critical infrastructure.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #165 - Anti-Poisoning Techniques
**Goal:** Prevent intentional corruption of AI training data or inputs.
**Mechanism:** Develop advanced detection systems for suspicious data patterns; create resilient training methodologies maintaining performance when data is compromised; establish recovery protocols for exposed systems.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #166 - Bug-Finding Democratization
**Goal:** Significantly improve security posture of critical infrastructure worldwide through government-subsidized access to advanced cybersecurity AI tools.
**Mechanism:** Procure subsidized access for banks, financial institutions, and vulnerable sectors to deploy bug-finding AI tools widely.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #167 - Comprehensive Infosec Framework
**Goal:** Create a multi-layered security approach addressing external human attackers, internal human threats, external model attackers, and internal model threats.
**Mechanism:** Build comprehensive AI-specific infosec architecture covering all threat vectors; develop specialized security measures tailored to AI systems (which can be surveilled more intensively than humans).
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #168 - Verified Software for Cyber Resilience
**Goal:** Develop software synthesis systems generating code with machine-checkable proofs satisfying functional, safety, and security specifications.
**Mechanism:** Address vulnerabilities in embedded systems (SCADA, medical devices, communication networks) through formal verification; decompose complex systems into verified components to reduce attack surface.
**Who's working on it:** Building on DARPA's I2O office work.

### #169 - AI for Formally Verified Cyberdefense
**Goal:** Rewrite software to be secure by default using formal verification methods.
**Mechanism:** Recruit cybersecurity experts from Google, NSA; create early warning systems for unusual exploitation patterns; develop hardened systems resistant to automated attacks; build resilient backup capabilities.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #170 - Protection of Global Security Weak Points
**Goal:** Strengthen weakest links in global security infrastructure to reduce overall attack surface.
**Mechanism:** Establish global security standards; fund security improvements in countries lacking resources; target jurisdictions where corruption enables easier access.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #171 - Fraud Prevention
**Goal:** Develop specialized tools and techniques preventing AI-enabled fraud across finance, identity verification, and digital communications.
**Mechanism:** Create detection mechanisms for synthetic media; develop authentication protocols resistant to AI impersonation; build monitoring systems for unusual patterns indicating fraudulent activity.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #172 - Honeypot Networks
**Goal:** Detect rogue AI systems using decoy systems.
**Mechanism:** Deploy decoy systems to attract and identify unauthorized AI activity; use gleaned information to inform other parts of the security effort.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #173 - Autoverification (Lean)
**Goal:** Develop systems that automate formal verification through Lean theorem proving, addressing the critical shortage of Lean programmers worldwide (estimated at "a few hundred").
**Mechanism:** Create automated tools for Lean enabling implementations of current AI architectures with much stronger formal guarantees.
**Who's working on it:** Renaissance Philanthropy referenced.

### #174 - Formal Verification of AI Hardware
**Goal:** Apply mathematical formal verification to critical AI components and surrounding infrastructure.
**Mechanism:** Focus verification on specific properties (memory safety, transaction timing, security boundaries); prioritize key computational infrastructure like Linux kernel or systems controlling electrical grids.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.

### #175 - Cyber Weapons for AI Disruption
**Goal:** Develop specialized cyberweapons designed to disrupt, sabotage, or shut down AI training runs.
**Mechanism:** Acquire GPUs or similar hardware to discover zero-day vulnerabilities specific to AI systems (similar to Stuxnet development by studying centrifuges); serve as deterrence and emergency intervention tools if intelligence explosion risks are detected.
**Who's working on it:** Not specified.
